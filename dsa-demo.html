Conversation Graph:
Inferring Communication Roles from Sentiment-Weighted Interactions

A custom doubly-linked-list sliding temporal window pairs with a directed weighted graph to model team communication from a transcript. Each utterance is scored with a VADER-style sentiment lexicon, edges inherit the average sentiment of the interactions they represent, and each speaker's role is inferred from in- and out-degree.

How it works

01 · WINDOW

Sliding Temporal Window

A doubly-linked list keeps every utterance from the last w seconds. As the transcript advances, old events are popped from the head in O(1) and new ones appended at the tail. The list is the conversational "short-term memory" — anyone still in it is considered an addressable peer.

02 · GRAPH

Directed Weighted Graph

Each new utterance creates a directed edge from the speaker to every other speaker still inside the window. Repeat interactions accumulate weight on the edge. Self-loops are dropped — speaking doesn't count as addressing yourself.

03 · SENTIMENT

VADER-style Scoring

Every utterance gets a compound sentiment score from a curated valence lexicon (with negation, punctuation, and capitalization handling). Each edge stores the running average of its underlying interactions; that average drives the edge color in the visualization.

04 · ROLES

Role Classification

Once the graph is built, each speaker's in-degree and out-degree are summed across all edges. Simple thresholds map the (in, out) pair to a role: Leader / Coordinator, Active Speaker, Frequently Addressed, or Observer.
